The Problem

Operators Are Flying Blind

Restaurant owner-operators are the backbone of the F&B industry. But vendors control the narrative — pricing is opaque, contracts are stacked, and operators have no independent source to compare notes.

Most operators overpay by 20–40% on key vendor contracts simply because they've never seen anyone else's numbers. That information gap costs real money, every single month.

"You can't negotiate what you can't benchmark. Operators need each other's data to make smarter decisions."

The Founder

Andrew Wang

6+ years in F&B — from commercial real estate to Botrista (food robotics) to Workstream, where he built the Asian restaurant go-to-market strategy serving 30,000+ locations. Today, he serves as SVP of Global Sales at Elaiksir, bringing automated specialty beverage solutions to cafés, restaurants, and hospitality brands — and as founder of MOM.

Inspired by Danny Meyer's "Setting the Table" philosophy, Andrew saw firsthand how vendors prey on operators who lack information. He started MOM to build the peer network and transparent data source he wished existed — by operators, for operators.

Bilingual in English and Chinese, Andrew bridges Asia-born concepts, local operators, landlords, and service partners in practical ways.

"I kept meeting restaurant owners who were paying wildly different prices for the same services. The only difference was who they knew. That's not okay."
